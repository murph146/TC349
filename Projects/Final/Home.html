R/C Helicopter Guide

Welcome to guide about remote control helicopters. Several years I began to take an interest in learning to build and fly r/c helicopters and have turned it into a personal hobby. I initially got into the r/c hobby back when I was in high school after my parents got me one for Christmas. At the time I didn't even realize how big the r/c industry was and the number of different kinds of r/c devices you can purchase. Now all this talk about r/c toys might have you thinking of the r/c cars or boats you got as a present when you were a kid, but I can assure you these are nothing like that. The majority of these type of helicopters are extremely powerful and have the ability to inflict serious damage on a person or animal if the proper precautions aren't taken.

Types of R/C Helicopters

  1. Gas Helicopters
    • Powered by a small engine and nitro fuel
    • Typically large in size
    • More expensive than electric models
    • Primarily for experienced fliers
  2. Electric Helicopters
    • Powered by electric motor and battery
    • Most popular
    • Prices more reasonable
    • Less noise and no fuel to deal with
    • More configuration choices (Co-Axil, Fixed, Collective, Mini, Micro)
    • Some can be flown indoors
