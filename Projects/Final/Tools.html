Once you choose the type of helicopter you want it will be important that you have a basic toolkit. Whether your at home or out flying having these tools with you will save you a lot of time and get you back out flying quickly. It's also important that you keep these tools organized and together because many of them are much smaller than a typical version. This is due to the fact that when working with most r/c devices the nuts, bolts, and screws are quite small.
Above are what I would consider the four most important types of tools to have when working on an remote control helicopter. However, these are just the tools I feel are necessary for getting started and building a basic toolkit. From left to right, the tools above are as follows: assorted mini pliers, allen wrenches, digital multi-meter, and assorted screw drivers. As you try out different helicopters or even other types of r/c devices these tools will come in very handy. Many of the big manufacturers use identical screws, nuts, and bolts so having these tools around is key.
